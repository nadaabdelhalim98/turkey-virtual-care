# Annotated Sources
## Virtual Care Readiness: A Framework for Turkey

All sources used in the analysis — with annotation on what each contributed and why it was selected.

---

### Peer-Reviewed Literature

**[1] Kario K et al.**
*Remote Blood Pressure Management and Systolic Blood Pressure Control.*
New England Journal of Medicine. 2021.
**Used for:** RPM efficacy evidence — 5–10 mmHg systolic BP reduction figure.
**Why selected:** NEJM RCT is the highest-quality evidence tier for the clinical evidence lens.

---

**[2] Blood Pressure Lowering Treatment Trialists' Collaboration.**
*Pharmacological blood pressure lowering for primary and secondary prevention of cardiovascular disease across different levels of blood pressure.*
The Lancet. 2021.
**Used for:** 10 mmHg reduction = 20% cardiovascular event reduction — ROI section.
**Why selected:** Lancet meta-analysis provides the clinical outcome justification for investment.

---

**[3] Flodgren G et al.**
*Telehealth interventions for people with chronic conditions.*
Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews. 2022.
**Used for:** 26% emergency admission reduction with remote monitoring. Non-inferiority of teleconsultation for stable chronic conditions.
**Why selected:** Cochrane reviews are the gold standard for systematic evidence synthesis.

---

**[4] Birinci S et al.**
*A Digital Opportunity for Patients to Manage Their Health: Turkey National Personal Health Record System (e-Nabız).*
Balkan Medical Journal / PMC. 2023.
DOI: 10.4274/balkanmedj.galenos.2023.2023-1-105
**Used for:** e-Nabız 82% population reach. 28.9% hypertensive medication non-adherence figure. Wearable integration capability.
**Why selected:** Only peer-reviewed English-language study specifically on e-Nabız system capabilities and patient data.

---

**[5] Doğan et al.**
*Controlling Hypertension in Turkey: Not a Hopeless Dream.*
PMC4089670. 2014.
**Used for:** 22.5 million hypertensives in Turkey. PatenT 2 study — 28.7% BP control rate.
**Why selected:** Most cited epidemiological source for Turkish hypertension prevalence data.

## Notes on Source Selection

**Intentional diversity of source types:** This analysis draws on peer-reviewed RCTs and systematic reviews (for clinical evidence), primary regulatory documents (for the regulatory lens), national digital statistics (for infrastructure readiness), and market intelligence (for commercial context). No single source type dominates.

**Recency:** All market and regulatory sources are from 2022 or later. The peer-reviewed clinical evidence (NEJM, Lancet, Cochrane) is from 2021–2022, reflecting the most recent high-quality trials. The hypertension epidemiology data (PatenT 2, WHO STEPS) predates 2020 but represents the most recent population-level Turkish survey available.

**Local sources:** The Balkan Medical Journal e-Nabız study and the PatenT 2 hypertension study are Turkey-specific peer-reviewed sources. The regulatory documents are primary Turkish government sources. This combination of global evidence with local context is intentional — it is what separates an applied framework from a generic literature review.
